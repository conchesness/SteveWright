Padding and Margins

A "row" has exactly 12 columns "cols". By numbering he cols, you can controll their size. You can use margins and padding to control how content is placed within a div. Padding on a div will push content against the edge of the div. Margins on a 'p' tag can do the same thing. You can use values 1-5. m-5 and p5 apply all around. mt/pt is top. mb/pb is bottom. ms/ps is start of left. me/pe is end of left. my/py is top and bottom. mx/px is right and left.



m-5
Margins on all 4 sides



my-5
margins top (mt) and bottom (mb)



ms-5
Margin on the left (start)



me-5
Margin on the right (end)

<div class="row border"> <!--col-4 defines this as 1/3 of a row.--> <div class="col-4 border"> </div> <div class="col-8 border"> <div class="row"> <!--col-6 defines each column as 1/2 a row--> <div class="col-6 border"> </div> <div class="col-6 border"> </div> <div class="col-6 border"> </div> <div class="col-6 border"> </div> </div> </div> </div>

Example: 13 Columns without any sizing: class="col"

If you do not give the col a number (ie col-4) then the contents of the column will dictate how many can fit in a row and it can be many more than 12.





















































<div class="row border"> <div class="col border"> </div> ... <div class="col border"> </div> </div>

Example: Exactly Three Columns: class="col-4"

A column is sperated in to 12 regions per row. You can designate the relative width of a column is by giving it a number between 1 and 12.













<div class="row border"> <div class="col-4 border"> </div> <div class="col-4 border"> </div> <div class="col-4 border"> </div> </div>

Example: "Responsive" Columns that break depending on screen size

Make the browser window smaller and see the the cells stack instead of in a row. Play with the breaks by screen size (sm, md, lg, xl, xxl) and with the relative size for example col-md-4. The purpose of this is to change the layout for different sized screens like phones.

































































































<div class="row border"> <!--In this case of empty cells, on an xxl screen there will be a row of 24 columns, on a lg screen there will be rows of 12 columns, 6 columns on a lg screen, 3 on a md screen, 2 on sm, and 1 on xs--> <div class="col-sm-6 col-md-4 col-lg-2 col-xl-1 col-xxl border"> </div> ... <div class="col-sm-6 col-md-4 col-lg-2 col-xl-1 col-xxl border"> </div> </div>

Responsive images

You can have an image change size as the page resizes with the 'img-fluid' class
<div class="row"> <div class="mb-5 col"> <img class="img-fluid" src="static/house-sunrise.jpg"> </div> </div>
